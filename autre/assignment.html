Analysis on chromosome 1

In order to study the correlation of the various markers with the quantitative trait were studying (IgG1 expression), well calculate a score based on the likelihood of observing an effect of the markers on the phenotype, the Log of Odd Ratios (LOD) score. This score is calculated as : \(LOD = \log_{10}\left( \frac{L_{H_1}(\hat{\mu}_0, \hat{\mu}_1, \hat{\sigma})}{L_{H_0}(\hat{\mu}, \hat{\sigma})} \right)\), \(L_{H_1}\) being the likelihood of correlation and \(L_{H_0}\) the likelihood of complete independence.

Conclusion

From our genetic data set on LEWIS and BN mice, we set out to find a Quantitative Trait Locus associated with G1 Immunoglobulin production.

With this aim, we studied genetic markers, DNA sequences whose locations are known on the two studied chromosomes (Chr1 and Chr2). By calculating Log of Odds Ratio scores for each markers, in order to find out the markers most correlated with higher IgG1 levels, we could narrow down potential QTL on both chromosomes.

Perspective

For simplicity, weve considered our organisms to be haploid, which is not too fallacious, considering BN and LEW mice are controlled inbred lineages, and practically homozygotes. To adapt our studies to a diploid consideration, we have to take into account the dominance relationships of our studied alleles. Therefore, wed have to measure LOD scores for the markers on both homologous chromosomes, and clear out the QTL.
